## Goal

Use a cooperative grip to study position, direction, centerline connection, and relaxed movement around pressure.

## Prerequisites

- Lesson 1 safety habits.
- Comfortable stance changes.
- Agreement to keep grip pressure light and instructional.

## Warm-up

- Shoulder and wrist mobility.
- Slow stepping while keeping the spine tall.
- Relax-and-reset drills for the arms.

## Technique links

- [Straight Hand 1](/techniques/straight-hand-1/)

## Solo review

Practice the straight-hand path without a partner. Keep the elbow connected, shoulders down, and stance stable before adding any grip context.

## Partner drill

Uke offers a light, steady contact. Nage enters slowly, pauses to check elbow line and posture, then resets. Switch roles often and avoid turning the drill into a contest.

## Safety focus

Do not yank against the grip or lock the elbow. Move slowly enough that both partners can feel structure before pressure increases.

## Review questions

- What changes when the shoulder rises?
- How does centerline support the arm?
- Why should the first grip drills stay cooperative?
