## Goal

Establish safe study habits before technique details: bow in, communicate clearly, move slowly, and know when to stop.

## Prerequisites

- A clear practice space.
- Agreement that all partner work stays cooperative.
- Basic awareness of tapping, verbal checks, and stopping on pain.

## Warm-up

- Gentle neck, shoulder, wrist, hip, knee, and ankle mobility.
- Easy stance changes with calm breathing.
- Low squats or kneeling transitions only as comfortable.

## Lesson flow

1. Open with bow-in, space checks, and expectations for slow cooperative practice.
2. Walk through stance, attention, and relaxed reset habits.
3. Rehearse tapping, verbal stops, and partner communication without applying techniques.
4. Close by showing where the next lessons and reference pages live on the site.

## Technique links

No technique page is required for this orientation lesson yet. Use the next lesson for the first falling reference.

## Solo review

Practice stepping into a ready stance, relaxing the shoulders, and resetting without rushing. Review the site navigation so practice notes, principles, and technique pages are easy to find.

## Partner drill

With a partner, rehearse the training conversation: confirm the intended movement, agree on speed, demonstrate a tap or verbal stop, then reset. Do not add locks or takedowns.

## Safety focus

Separate learning from resistance. If either partner is confused, tense, or feeling sharp pain, stop and restart more slowly.

## Review questions

- What should you check before partner practice?
- When should uke tap, step, or speak?
- What is the difference between reviewing a sequence and applying force?
