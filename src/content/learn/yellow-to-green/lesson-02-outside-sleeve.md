## Goal

Study how the outside sleeve family changes the angle of the grabbing hand, captures structure, and breaks posture.

## Prerequisites

- Yellow-to-Green Lesson 1 sleeve-grab safety.
- Comfortable forward roll or controlled descent practice.
- Familiarity with circular motion, wrist rotation, and body drop as ideas.

## Warm-up

- Wrist circles and gentle forearm mobility.
- Step-pivot patterns around an imagined grip.
- Cooperative sleeve holds with quick verbal safety checks.

## Technique links

- [Outside Sleeve 1](/techniques/outside-sleeve-1/)
- [Outside Sleeve 2](/techniques/outside-sleeve-2/)
- [Outside Sleeve 3](/techniques/outside-sleeve-3/)

## Solo review

Walk the outside circular path for each technique family member. Emphasize foot placement, hip turn, and level change before thinking about the lock.

## Partner drill

Uke gives a steady outside sleeve grip. Nage performs the entry, pauses when the hand is captured, and adds only enough rotation to show posture change. Reset before any sharp pressure.

## Safety focus

Do not crank the wrist. Uke should be able to move, tap, or speak before pressure reaches the elbow or shoulder.

## Review questions

- What makes Outside Sleeve 1 different from the later outside sleeve references?
- Where should the captured hand be relative to nage's center?
- How does body drop differ from pulling down with the arms?
