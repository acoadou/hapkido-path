## Goal

Study same-side sleeve-grab entries as a family, then use the technique pages to compare details.

## Prerequisites

- Outside sleeve review at slow speed.
- Clear tapping and partner communication.
- Enough falling skill for any instructor-approved descent.

## Warm-up

- Wrist, elbow, and shoulder mobility.
- Centerline stepping with relaxed arms.
- Slow hand-capture patterns without joint pressure.

## Technique links

- [Sleeve 1-2](/techniques/sleeve-1-2/)
- [Sleeve 3](/techniques/sleeve-3/)
- [Sleeve 4](/techniques/sleeve-4/)
- [Sleeve 5-6](/techniques/sleeve-5-6/)

## Solo review

Practice the entry shapes in the air and name the intended control point for each one. Keep the shoulders low and reset after every repetition.

## Partner drill

Uke gives a cooperative same-side sleeve grip. Nage performs one entry at a time, freezes at the control point, and asks uke whether the posture line is clear before continuing.

## Safety focus

Same-side sleeve locks can load the wrist before posture is broken. Build pressure gradually and stop immediately on sharp pain.

## Review questions

- Which mechanics are shared across Sleeve 1-2, 3, 4, and 5-6?
- What makes uke's posture change before the finish?
- How can nage practice the entry solo without pretending to learn the lock solo?
