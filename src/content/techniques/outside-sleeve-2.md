## Short reminder

Create the opening with a relaxed circle, secure the gripping hand, and turn Uke's elbow line away from a strong postural position.

## Step-by-step breakdown

1. Receive the grab without tensing the shoulder.
2. Step to a safe angle and circle the grabbed arm.
3. Secure the hand and guide the elbow line into a weak direction.
4. Use a small body drop to take posture.
5. Finish with a controlled pin or reset for drilling.

## Key details

- The angle matters more than speed.
- Keep Uke's elbow from folding into a strong frame.
- Practice the transition on both sides.

## Common mistakes

- Turning before the hand is secured.
- Leaning with the head instead of moving the feet.
- Forgetting to check Uke's balance before the finish.

## Safety notes

Work at low intensity and stop as soon as the wrist or elbow feels trapped.
