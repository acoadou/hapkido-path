## Short reminder

- Uke grabs Tori’s outside sleeve.
- Tori uses a large outside circle to expose the gripping hand.
- Tori captures and sandwiches the hand.
- Tori twists the wrist outward and downward.
- Tori drops weight to break posture.
- Tori follows to a controlled pin.

## Step-by-step breakdown

1. Settle posture and avoid pulling straight back against the grip.
2. Draw a large outside circle so the gripping hand turns palm-up or becomes visible.
3. Cover the hand with the free hand and sandwich it against your grabbed arm.
4. Align the wrist rotation with Uke’s elbow line rather than cranking sideways.
5. Drop weight through your stance while keeping the connection close to center.
6. Follow Uke down and stabilize before releasing pressure.

## Key details

- Move your feet so your shoulders can stay relaxed.
- Keep the grip connected to your center, not floating at arm’s length.
- Let the body turn create the wrist angle.

## Common mistakes

- Pulling straight backward against the grip
- Letting Uke’s hand drift away from the centerline
- Twisting with the fingers only instead of using the whole body
- Dropping weight without maintaining the wrist connection
- Releasing the control after the takedown

## Safety notes

- Apply wrist pressure slowly.
- Uke should tap or verbally signal early.
- Avoid sudden force on the wrist, elbow, or shoulder.
