## Simple explanation

Safe falling is the habit of receiving motion without bracing with a straight arm, striking the head, or twisting into the floor.

## Why it matters

Many Hapkido techniques require uke to move, kneel, roll, or fall. Falling skill keeps cooperative practice safer and lets joint-lock practice stay slow instead of panicked.

## How it appears in techniques

Look for the moment when uke chooses a safe step, kneel, roll, or descent rather than resisting pressure until balance is completely lost.

## Common mistake

A common error is treating falling as separate from technique practice. Uke should begin protecting posture, neck, and limbs as soon as balance starts to change.

## Mini-drill placeholder

Practice kneeling descents, low rolls, and technical stand-ups before connecting them to locks or takedowns.
