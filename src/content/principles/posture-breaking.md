## Short explanation

Posture breaking means changing uke's base, spine angle, shoulder line, or ability to step before trying to finish.

## Why it matters

A lock applied to a stable person becomes a strength contest. A lock applied after posture breaks needs less force and gives uke clearer feedback.

## Physical feeling

Nage should feel uke's balance start to move before adding more pressure. Uke should feel the body following the joint line rather than a sudden painful crank.

## Bad version vs good version

| Bad version | Good version |
|---|---|
| Forcing the finish against a stable stance | Changing angle and base first |
| Pain before movement | Movement before sharp pain |

## Mini-drill

From a light sleeve grip, slowly turn the wrist line until uke has to take one step. Stop before finishing the technique.
