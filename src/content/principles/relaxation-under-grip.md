## Short explanation

Relaxation under grip means keeping enough structure to move while avoiding the rigid tension that makes the grip stronger.

## Why it matters

A tense arm often pulls directly against uke's strongest line. A relaxed but connected arm can move in a circle, turn the body, and find a better angle.

## Physical feeling

The shoulder stays down, the elbow can move, and the feet remain available. Relaxation is not collapse; it is usable structure without panic.

## Bad version vs good version

| Bad version | Good version |
|---|---|
| Freezing or yanking | Breathing, settling, and moving around the grip |
| Limp arm with no structure | Relaxed arm connected to torso and feet |

## Mini-drill

Have uke hold a sleeve lightly. Nage breathes, checks the shoulder, and makes a slow circle without trying to escape.
