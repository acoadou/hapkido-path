## Simple explanation

Keeping important actions close to the central line of the body for structure and control. Practice it as a repeatable mechanic before treating any technique as a fixed sequence.

## Why it matters

This principle helps smaller movements become clearer, safer, and less dependent on strength.

## How it appears in techniques

Look for the moment where posture, angle, or connection changes before the visible finish happens.

## Common mistake

Students often rush to the end shape and miss the body organization that makes the movement work.

## Mini-drill placeholder

Practice the mechanic with cooperative pressure, then pause and describe what changed in balance, elbow position, or wrist angle.
